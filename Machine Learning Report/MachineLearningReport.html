Abstract

As technology advances, people are always looking for a simpler and more efficient way to perform tasks such as using AI in the medical field. This research studies the relationship of the variables that may affect the accuracy of the output of a machine learning model that tries to identify cancer cells. There is a total of 3300 images comprised of 500 different endometrial specimens to be identified in this project, funded by the National Basic Research Program of China. The results of the research show that there is little correlation between the learning rate and the accuracy since plots all show that the accuracy becomes unstable no matter how much the learning rate is changed. However, there is some correlation between momentum and accuracy as the higher the momentum, the more stable the accuracy becomes. Endometrial cancer is a type of cancer that is highly curable if patients are diagnosed early. With the help of this research (finding out what increases the accuracy), it can help further advance medical technology.

Research Question

What variables can affect the stability of the accuracy when using a machine learning model to identify cancer cells?

Hypothesis

Null Hypothesis: The variables (learning rate and/or momentum) do not affect the stability of the accuracy when identifying cancer cells

Alternative Hypothesis: The variables (learning rate and/or) affect the stability of the accuracy when identifying cancer cells

Learning rate: 0.01 Momentum: 0.95 | Accuracy is unstable

Learning rate: 0.45 Momentum: 0.95 | Accuracy is unstable

Learning rate: 0.01 Momentum: 0.45 | Accuracy is unstable during the first few epochs

Learning rate: 0.01 Momentum: 1 | Accuracy relatively stable

Discussion Conclusion Further Directions Next Steps

From the plots, we can conclude that the learning rate and accuracy of the model do not have much correlation. In the first graph, the learning rate was set to 0.01 and the momentum was set to 0.95, while in the second graph, the learning rate was set to 0.45 and the momentum stayed the same. Despite the difference in the learning rate, both graphs show that the accuracy is unstable. This shows that there is little to no correlation between the learning rate and accuracy. We can also conclude that the momentum and accuracy have some sort of correlation. In the third graph, the learning rate was set to 0.01 and the momentum to 0.45, while the momentum was changed to 1 for the fourth graph. The results of the plot show that the fourth graph has a more stable accuracy growth rate as the accuracy never drops as the number of epochs increases. If we extract all the results we have concluded, it can be seen that half of them were supported by the null hypothesis and half of them were supported by the alternative hypothesis. However, this doesn’t mean the project would end here. This conclusion is only the first step to developing this project. This research project can be further expanded since if the accuracy of the model increases to about 90%, it will help identify those that have endometrial cancer earlier and can help them start their treatments earlier.
